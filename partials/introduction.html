About cSpeed
cSpeed tests the quality of your browsing experience by directly measuring how much time it takes to load a list of popular Web pages. cSpeed does not collect any personal Web-browsing information. We do record your IP address, ISP, city, and browser user-agent (which includes your operating system and browser version). We also run a small computational test to measure javascript performance.

cSpeed was developed by Xuefeng Zhu, Ankit Singla, and P. Brighten Godfrey, computer scientists at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. This work is part of a larger speed-of-light Internet project with our collaborators Bala Chandrasekaran and Bruce Maggs at Duke University. The first step, of course, is to measure how fast the Internet is today. We developed cSpeed with twin objectives: (a) to measure Internet speed across a diverse set of users; and (b) to provide you a way of comparing Internet service options in your area. Unlike other 'speed tests', cSpeed directly evaluates your browsing experience by loading Web sites, instead of measuring bandwidth. Learn even more.
